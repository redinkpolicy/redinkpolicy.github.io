Inflation · BLS CPI, May 2026

Inflation hits 4.2% — a three-year high

Before anyone blames a party: the BLS says energy drove more than 60% of the jump, and gas is up 40.5% in a year. Strip energy out and the rest runs 2.9%. It's a foreign oil shock, not 2021 — and the answer is producing more energy at home.

Inflation · BEA PCE, May 2026 (released Jun 25)

The Fed's preferred gauge runs hot too

Core PCE — the measure the Fed actually targets, with energy and food stripped out — came in at 3.4%, headline at 4.1%, both the highest since 2023. That's underlying inflation near double the Fed's 2% goal. Translation: rate cuts aren't coming on this data.

The Fed · FOMC, June 17, 2026

The Fed holds rates — and signals higher, not lower

The dot plot moved the wrong way for borrowers: the year-end projection rose from 3.4% to 3.8%. Markets bet on cuts; the Fed took the other side because inflation reaccelerated. And every notch up adds to the ~$1 trillion-a-year interest bill on the national debt.

Energy · U.S. DOE, June 23, 2026

DOE backs a $17.5B loan to restart nuclear construction

A conditional commitment of up to $17.5 billion targets long-lead parts for as many as 10 AP1000 reactors, aiming to cut timelines by up to three years. The right read: nuclear's problem was never safety — it's build cost and a supply chain we let rot. This goes at the actual bottleneck.

Education · New York Fed, May 2026

Student-loan defaults surge after the pause

About 1 million defaulted in late 2025 and 2.6 million more in early 2026 — with a second wave of ~7 million former SAVE borrowers loading. The hard truth: another pause doesn't fix this, it just moves the cliff and shifts the cost to taxpayers.

Taxes · IRS Statistics of Income (evergreen)

Who actually pays federal income tax?

The top 10% pay 70.5% of it; the bottom 50% pay 3.3%; about 30% of filers owe nothing. Payroll and sales taxes flatten the total burden — but on the most progressive tax we have, the top already carry it.

Education · Georgetown CEW (evergreen)

Is a college degree still worth it?

A bachelor's earns about $2.8 million over a lifetime vs $1.6 million for high school — roughly 75% more. But the average hides huge variance: the major and the occupation often matter more than the diploma itself.

Energy · Our World in Data (evergreen)

Is nuclear power actually safe?

By deaths per terawatt-hour, nuclear (~0.03) is the second-safest source and roughly 800× safer than coal (~24.6) — even counting Chernobyl and Fukushima. The real obstacle is build cost and time, not safety.
